🔵 Lead Funnel Management
New Stage
All leads that have not been touched. Ideally, every business in your territory.
  • When ready to engage, add to appropriate sequence and complete tasks to engage prospect
Min: 100 New Leads · Target: 200
In Progress
All leads you are actively pursuing (in a sequence).
  • Complete outreach tasks or any ad hoc outreach to connect with prospect
Min: 75 In Progress Leads
Connected
Leads you have made contact with and are actively trying to book a discovery/demo with.
  • Connect with decision maker to qualify the opportunity
Target: 30+ Connected Leads
Qualified
Leads you have done a qualification/discovery with and have qualified as an opportunity to add to your deal pipeline.
  • Complete a qualification and book in discovery/demo meeting
Min: 5/week · Target: 10/week
Unqualified
Leads disqualified on the basis of not being ICP.
  • Move lead to Unqualified Lead stage and add a note explaining why they are not worth chasing
Cold
Leads not disqualified but not currently worth chasing — unresponsive after 10 attempts, bad timing but ICP, or similar.
  • Move to Cold stage and add a task to re-engage when appropriate with notes on context
🚀 Deal Pipeline Management
Full Demo Booked
A qualified lead with a discovery/demo meeting on the calendar, actively pursuing to close in the near term.
  • Schedule meeting with prospect (if needed)
  • Add line items to reflect estimated MRR of deal
  • Update Close Date for estimated timeline
Min: 5/week · Target: 10/week
Solution Proposed
Demo completed to align solution to pain points. A partnership proposal has been made but not necessarily sent.
  • Book next steps: follow-up meeting, send invoice etc.
  • Update line items and Close Date if needed
Invoice Sent
A Deal Manager deal has been created and sent to prospect.
  • Book next meeting to walk through KYC and typeform
  • Use this meeting as a closing call in case they don't pay the invoice
Package Purchased
Invoice has been paid and the deal is ready to be handed off to the PSS/AM team.
  • Complete the Deal Handover Checklist (see Deal Handover tab)

Core Principle

  • You hold all the solution information — the prospect holds all the problem information
  • Do not give away solution info early. Collect problem info first to stay in control of the conversation
  • The more problem info you gather before revealing solutions, the more excited the prospect becomes to learn more
  • During discovery, explore four key categories in this order: ⚙️ Business Efficiency → 📈 Revenue Growth → 🛡️ Revenue Protection → 💰 Cost Comparison
  • Aim for 3–5 confirmed business problems (minimum 3) that Fresha can clearly solve — confirm they are a priority to solve now
  • Discovery meetings should be at least 30 minutes
⚖️ Why Problem-First Matters
❌ Give Solution Info Too Early
  • →Prospect holds all the power in the conversation
  • →They stop returning your calls
  • →Resistant to confirming next steps
  • →Deal goes cold — ghosted
  • →Uncertainty on your weekly pipeline commits
✅ Collect Problem Info First
  • →You control the conversation and drive next steps
  • →Prospect is keen to book follow-ups
  • →Genuine excitement about solving their problems
  • →Increased desire and urgency to move forward
  • →You position yourself as a credible industry expert
🗺️ Discovery Meeting Structure
1
Agenda
Set the stage with a clear "What's in it for you" statement — typically focused on increasing business efficiency and growing revenues. Briefly explain what Fresha is and outline what you'll cover. This earns engagement before the real conversation begins.
2
⚙️
Business Efficiency
Where is the business losing time to manual or admin-heavy processes? Scheduling, staff management, client comms, reporting. Quantify the hours and cost of that drag. Questions to be added.
3
📈
Revenue Growth
Where is growth being left on the table? New client acquisition, rebooking rates, retail upsell, marketing reach. Are their current tools helping or hindering their ability to grow? Questions to be added.
4
🛡️
Revenue Protection
Where is revenue leaking from the business? No-shows, last-minute cancellations, manual errors, unbilled services. What does that cost them monthly? Questions to be added.
5
💰
Cost Comparison
Surface the true cost of their current setup — software fees, processing rates, add-on charges. Build an honest comparison so they can see the value clearly. Often the most powerful hook. Questions to be added.
6
Summary & Next Steps
Recap the 3–5 problems you've uncovered and confirm with the prospect that solving them is a priority now. Lock in the next meeting before you leave the call.
💡 Reminder: This session is about collecting problem information — not presenting solutions. Save the solution reveal for the demo/proposal stage.
📐 How to Quantify Every Problem

For every business problem surfaced, dig into all four dimensions before moving on. Vague problems don't close deals — specific, quantified ones do.

❓
What
What exactly is the problem? What does it look like in practice?
🤔
Why
Why does this happen? What causes it? Why hasn't it been fixed?
👤
Who
Who manages this? Who is affected? Who makes the decision to fix it?
📊
Impact
How much time, money, or stress does this cost? How often does it happen?
