🔵 Lead Funnel Management
New Stage
All leads that have not been touched. Ideally, every business in your territory.
  • When ready to engage, add to appropriate sequence and complete tasks to engage prospect
Min: 100 New Leads · Target: 200
In Progress
All leads you are actively pursuing (in a sequence).
  • Complete outreach tasks or any ad hoc outreach to connect with prospect
Min: 75 In Progress Leads
Connected
Leads you have made contact with and are actively trying to book a discovery/demo with.
  • Connect with decision maker to qualify the opportunity
Target: 30+ Connected Leads
Qualified
Leads you have done a qualification/discovery with and have qualified as an opportunity to add to your deal pipeline.
  • Complete a qualification and book in discovery/demo meeting
Min: 5/week · Target: 10/week
Unqualified
Leads disqualified on the basis of not being ICP.
  • Move lead to Unqualified Lead stage and add a note explaining why they are not worth chasing
Cold
Leads not disqualified but not currently worth chasing — unresponsive after 10 attempts, bad timing but ICP, or similar.
  • Move to Cold stage and add a task to re-engage when appropriate with notes on context
🚀 Deal Pipeline Management
Full Demo Booked
A qualified lead with a discovery/demo meeting on the calendar, actively pursuing to close in the near term.
  • Schedule meeting with prospect (if needed)
  • Add line items to reflect estimated MRR of deal
  • Update Close Date for estimated timeline
Min: 5/week · Target: 10/week
Solution Proposed
Demo completed to align solution to pain points. A partnership proposal has been made but not necessarily sent.
  • Book next steps: follow-up meeting, send invoice etc.
  • Update line items and Close Date if needed
Invoice Sent
A Deal Manager deal has been created and sent to prospect.
  • Book next meeting to walk through KYC and typeform
  • Use this meeting as a closing call in case they don't pay the invoice
Package Purchased
Invoice has been paid and the deal is ready to be handed off to the PSS/AM team.
  • Complete the Deal Handover Checklist (see Deal Handover tab)

👋 Introduction
Goals
  • ✓Build rapport
  • ✓Establish credibility
  • ✓Earn (don't ask for) permission to ask the next question
Upbeat & friendly Casual & conversational Concise Avoid anchoring early
💬 Example Script

Open: "Is Greg there?"

Intro: "Hey Greg, this is Brian Donley calling from Fresha." — "You're calling from where?" — "I'm calling from Fresha — have you heard of us?"

Why calling: "We're the world's most-used booking software for beauty and wellness businesses. I'm the manager in Toronto and I wanted to give you a quick call to see if what we offer might be able to help in any way."

💬 Engage
Qualifying Questions
  • "I noticed that you're working with Booker — is that right? How long have you been with them? Have you always used them?"
  • "Do you remember what made you guys choose Booker originally?"
  • "I've worked with a ton of salons that have moved to us from Booker to eliminate those day-to-day headaches — if you had to rate Booker out of 10, what would you give them? Why? What would make it a 10?"
  • "Running a salon can be hectic — what's taking up most of your energy right now?"
Keep questions open-ended Let them talk Steer the conversation Use social proof
Probing Questions — tell me more about that
Example: they say "We set up Booker to get more bookings online"
  • →"What percentage of your clients book online?"
  • →"How do the other clients book?"
  • →"Who picks up the phone when a client calls to book?"
  • →"How full is your calendar currently?"
  • →"How does the calendar look for all of your staff?"
  • →"Where do you get most of your new clients from?"
Go deeper Let them talk Steer
🛡️ Handle
Brush-offs — reflexive responses
"Happy with our software" "Not interested" "Send me an email"
Acknowledge "Totally fair"
Reframe "I'm not here to push any changes today"
Ask "I noticed you're working with Booker — is that right? How long have you been with them?"
Non-confrontational Low pressure Easy-to-answer questions
Objections — legitimate concerns
"In a contract" "Sounds expensive" "Bad experience switching"
Acknowledge "Totally fair, change can be tough"
Clarify "Do you mind sharing what made the switch so difficult?"
Solve "We focus on building successful long-term partnerships, which is why we don't rush the switch — we have a dedicated team to handle the migration from end-to-end."
🎯 Close
Goals
  • ✓Recap the challenges they've shared and how Fresha might help
  • ✓Confirm it's worth exploring further
  • ✓Book a deeper discovery — make the ask low-commitment
💡 Note: Don't ask if they're 100% committed — most people who end up closing would say no at this stage. Keep it light.

Objective

  • Show how specific Fresha features solve business-critical pain points for the prospect — connect capabilities directly to their needs
  • Every demo should be focused, engaging, and personalised — not a generic walkthrough
  • Frame the demo around 2–4 pain points from discovery and get the prospect to commit to moving forward if those points are addressed
🧭 4 Principles for a Successful Demo
🔗
Continuity

Focus only on features that directly address the prospect's challenges. Use their own language throughout:

  • →"As you said…"
  • →"You mentioned before that [pain point]…"
  • →"Based on what you shared earlier…"
  • →"Is this addressing the challenge you mentioned about [pain point]?"
💬
Two-Way Engagement

Invite the prospect into the conversation throughout — never let it become a one-sided presentation:

  • →"How does this look so far?"
  • →"Is this addressing your need for [challenge]?"
  • →Before demoing a feature, ask how they currently handle it in their setup
  • →Reflect back what they say to show you're listening
🤝
Pain-Point Commitment

Get the prospect to pre-commit before the demo even starts:

  • →"Today's demo will focus on how we can help you solve [pain points 1, 2, 3]. If we address these effectively, would you feel ready to discuss next steps?"
  • →Throughout: "If our system can help you reduce no-shows by X%, would that meet your needs?"
  • →Before closing: "If we can deliver on these three points — [summarise] — would that make a strong case for moving forward?"
🎯
Confirm Next Steps

Never end without securing a clear commitment to move forward:

  • →Recap how Fresha addresses their pain points
  • →Check for final questions before moving on
  • →Don't fear a 'no' — engage objections and reinforce value
  • →Schedule the next meeting: Cost Comparison or Sign-Up
🗺️ Demo Call Structure
1
What is Fresha?
Greet, build rapport, and reintroduce the Fresha ecosystem. Set the agenda, outline what you'll cover, and give a brief overview of next steps if Fresha is a good fit. Show enthusiasm — your energy sets the tone.
2
Pain Points Recap
Recap the top 2–4 pain points from discovery. These guide the entire structure of the platform demo. Get confirmation: "From our previous conversation, I understand that [X and Y] are your top priorities — does that sound right?"
3
Platform Demo
Demo core features plus any additional features tailored to the prospect's specific pain points. Before showing a feature, ask how they handle it today. Check in throughout.
Core Features (most demos)
  • 📅 Calendar / appointment management
  • 👥 Service menu / client lists
  • 🛒 Marketplace / customer booking flow
  • 💻 Online bookings & deposits
  • 📣 Marketing
  • 💳 Fresha Pay
  • 📊 Reporting
Additional (tailor to prospect)
  • 💰 Team pay
  • 📈 Insights
  • ⭐ Google ratings boost
  • ⏳ Waitlists
  • 📌 Sticky bookings
  • 🛍️ Shop / inventory
4
Partner Onboarding Journey
Walk the prospect through what happens next: Set-Up Meeting → ASC → Team Training → Go-Live → Ongoing Account Management. Highlight the support Fresha provides at every stage.
5
Package Proposal
Share onboarding packages, pricing, and add-ons. Suggest the package most relevant to the business and recommend add-ons based on their specific needs. Request any invoices or statements needed for a cost comparison.
6
Schedule Next Steps
Confirm the prospect is happy and agree on a timeline. Check if other stakeholders need to be involved. Book either a Cost Comparison call or Sign-Up meeting and send the post-demo summary email.
💡 Reminder: If the prospect agrees Fresha solves their pain points, move straight to next steps at the end of the demo. If they hesitate, a Cost Comparison, Final Q&A, or Package Negotiation meeting may follow.

Open Periscope ↗

Core Principle

  • You hold all the solution information — the prospect holds all the problem information
  • Do not give away solution info early. Collect problem info first to stay in control of the conversation
  • The more problem info you gather before revealing solutions, the more excited the prospect becomes to learn more
  • During discovery, explore four key categories in this order: ⚙️ Business Efficiency → 📈 Revenue Growth → 🛡️ Revenue Protection → 💰 Cost Comparison
  • Aim for 3–5 confirmed business problems (minimum 3) that Fresha can clearly solve — confirm they are a priority to solve now
  • Discovery meetings should be at least 30 minutes
⚖️ Why Problem-First Matters
❌ Give Solution Info Too Early
  • →Prospect holds all the power in the conversation
  • →They stop returning your calls
  • →Resistant to confirming next steps
  • →Deal goes cold — ghosted
  • →Uncertainty on your weekly pipeline commits
✅ Collect Problem Info First
  • →You control the conversation and drive next steps
  • →Prospect is keen to book follow-ups
  • →Genuine excitement about solving their problems
  • →Increased desire and urgency to move forward
  • →You position yourself as a credible industry expert
🗺️ Discovery Meeting Structure
1
Agenda
Set the stage with a clear "What's in it for you" statement — typically focused on increasing business efficiency and growing revenues. Briefly explain what Fresha is and outline what you'll cover. This earns engagement before the real conversation begins.
2
⚙️
Business Efficiency
Where is the business losing time to manual or admin-heavy processes? Scheduling, staff management, client comms, reporting. Quantify the hours and cost of that drag. Questions to be added.
3
📈
Revenue Growth
Where is growth being left on the table? New client acquisition, rebooking rates, retail upsell, marketing reach. Are their current tools helping or hindering their ability to grow? Questions to be added.
4
🛡️
Revenue Protection
Where is revenue leaking from the business? No-shows, last-minute cancellations, manual errors, unbilled services. What does that cost them monthly? Questions to be added.
5
💰
Cost Comparison
Surface the true cost of their current setup — software fees, processing rates, add-on charges. Build an honest comparison so they can see the value clearly. Often the most powerful hook. Questions to be added.
6
Summary & Next Steps
Recap the 3–5 problems you've uncovered and confirm with the prospect that solving them is a priority now. Lock in the next meeting before you leave the call.
💡 Reminder: This session is about collecting problem information — not presenting solutions. Save the solution reveal for the demo/proposal stage.
📐 How to Quantify Every Problem

For every business problem surfaced, dig into all four dimensions before moving on. Vague problems don't close deals — specific, quantified ones do.

❓
What
What exactly is the problem? What does it look like in practice?
🤔
Why
Why does this happen? What causes it? Why hasn't it been fixed?
👤
Who
Who manages this? Who is affected? Who makes the decision to fix it?
📊
Impact
How much time, money, or stress does this cost? How often does it happen?
